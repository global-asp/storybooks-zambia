Ifuku dimu, mama wasendeli nyikabu yayivulu nankashi.

One day, mama got a lot of fruit.

Ubushiku bumo bamaayo balikwete ifisabo ifingi.

“Tukadaku nyikabu ifukwinyi?” twehwili. “Tukuda nyikabu wufuku,” nindi mama.

“When can we have some fruit?” we ask. “We will have the fruit tonight,” says mama.

Twalipwishe, “Ni lilali tuleelyako ifisabo?” Bamaayo abati, “Tuleelya ifisabo ubushiku.”

Manakwami Rahim wachifwa. Watompan’ga nyikabu yezhima. Wadan’ga yayivulu.

My brother Rahim is greedy. He tastes all the fruit. He eats a lot of it.

Ndume yandi Rahim alifunuka. Aletoma fyonse ifisabo. Alile ifingi.

“Tala zheliliyi Rahim!” manakwami wamunyanya wabidikili. “Rahim wamanya na chifwa,” nahoshi.

“Look at what Rahim did!” shouts my little brother. “Rahim is naughty and selfish,” I say.

“Moneni ifyo Rahim acitile.” kandume yandi kapunda. Nayasuka ati, “Rahim toomfwa kabili aliitemwa.”

Mama nahili na Rahim.

Mother is angry with Rahim.

Bamaayo balifulilwe Rahim saana.

Tunataminini na Rahim ilan’ga Rahim koti kudanakena.

We are also angry with Rahim. But Rahim is not sorry.

Bonse naifwe twalifulilwe Rahim. Nomba Rahim tasakamene.

“Hiwukubabesha Rahim wanyi?” manakwami wamunyanya wehwili.

“Aren’t you going to punish Rahim?” asks little brother.

Akamwaice kandi kaipwishe ati, “Tamwamukande Rahim.”

“Rahim, ken’ga wukudanakena,” mama wamulezheli

“Rahim, soon you will be sorry,” warns mama.

Bamaayo bamusokele abati, “Calakubipila nomba line.”

Rahim watachikili kutiya kukata.

Rahim starts to feel sick.

Rahim atampile ukumfwa ukulwala.

“mwivumu dami mudi yilonda,” nindi Rahim.

“My tummy is so sore,” whispers Rahim.

Rahim atotosha ati, “Ulufumo lwandi lwalakalipa.”

Mama welikili nindi izhi zhikumwekana. Mukabu wunakubabesha Rahim!

Mama knew this would happen. The fruit is punishing Rahim!

Bamaayo balishibe ukuti ifi filecitika. Ifisabo fileekanda Rahim.

Chihahitili kapinji, Rahim wadanakeneni kudi etu. “Hinikamba kekala na chifwa chen’giku,” wadishikili. Kaha nawa twamukuhweleleli.

Later, Rahim says sorry to us. “I will never be so greedy again,” he promises. And we all believe him.

Panuma, Rahim alombele ubwelelo kuli ifwe. “Nshakafunuke nakabili.”Alaya. Bonse twalimusuminishe.

Illustrated by: Melany Pietersen
Translated by: Oscar Zangata
Language: Lunda
